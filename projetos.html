BMI is recognized as an international standard for assessing the degree of overweight and obesity. It is calculated by dividing weight (in kg) by height squared (in metres). BMI is the acronym for Body Mass Index, a parameter adopted by the World Health Organization to calculate the ideal weight of each person. The index is calculated as follows: the patient's weight is divided by their height squared. An individual is said to have normal weight when the BMI result is between 18.5 and 24.9.
